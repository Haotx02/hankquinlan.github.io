Research Projects

Football Club Valuation Model

As part of a graduate research project at Cornell, I led the design and development of a Python-based valuation system for evaluating the financial and operational health of European football clubs. I managed a cross-functional team, coordinated tasks, and implemented advanced financial techniques such as Discounted Cash Flow and P/E analysis.

To structure the system, I used SysML, context diagrams, and use case modeling to clarify user needs and design architecture. The model integrated real-time financial data and provided dynamic visualizations for investor-facing insights. This tool enables data-driven decisions for both managerial and investment purposes, significantly improving transparency and forecasting accuracy in the sports finance domain.

Workplace Diversity Simulation

In this simulation study, I developed a Continuous-Time Markov Chain (CTMC) model to assess how diversity-oriented HR policies affect workplace composition. The model captured recruitment, promotion, and exit flows of employees, simulating a wide range of policy interventions.

I evaluated scenarios such as female-prioritized promotion, diversity-based hiring, and external senior recruitment under structural barriers. The simulation revealed nuanced interactions between policy combinations, offering strategic insights for inclusive organizational design.
